## THE PROBLEM

The Town of Yarrow Point (via Town Attorney Emily Romanenko at OMW Law) requires
proof that the existing dock/boat ramp was **legally established** before the
Shoreline Management Act (SMA) took effect. Without this proof, they recommend
applying for a variance (YPMC Chapter 17.28).

### What the Town Attorney Accepts as Evidence

Per her April 1, 2026 letter:
- **Date-stamped photos** showing the dock/boat ramp existed **prior to 1972**
  (when the SMA was enacted)
- Photos must also show the dock in its **current configuration** (if it's now
  larger than the original, and there are no permits authorizing expansion,
  nonconforming status may be lost)

### What the Town Attorney Has Rejected

1. **The 1995 shoreline permit** noting "existing boat ramp" - she says this only
   authorized construction of the *second* dock, not the original
2. **The 2000 building permit** showing "dock/boat ramp" - same argument, it only
   authorized the new house
3. **Grainy aerial imagery** provided so far - "extremely grainy and difficult to
   decipher"
4. **Town's failure to object in 1995/2000** - per *Mercer Island v. Steinmann*,
   municipalities are not estopped from enforcing zoning by prior inaction

## LEGAL FRAMEWORK

### In Your Favor

#### RCW 90.58.270 - Pre-1969 Structures Protected
> "Nothing in this section constitutes authority for requiring or ordering the
> removal of any structures, improvements, docks, fills, or developments placed
> in navigable waters prior to December 4, 1969."

The state grants consent to the "retention and maintenance" of such structures.

**Caveat:** Does not apply to structures "placed on tidelands, shorelands, or beds
underlying said waters which are in trespass or in violation of state statutes."

#### WAC 173-27-080 - Nonconforming Development Standards
- Structures lawfully established before the SMA/SMP can continue as legal
  nonconforming structures
- May be maintained and repaired
- If damaged (up to 75% of replacement cost), can be reconstructed to prior
  configuration within 2 years

#### The 1995 & 2000 Permits as Circumstantial Evidence
While the Town Attorney argues these don't *prove* legal establishment, they are
strong circumstantial evidence that the Town was aware of and acquiesced to the
dock's existence. The planners who reviewed these applications saw the dock and
did not require its removal.

### Against You (Town's Arguments)

#### Taylor v. Stevens County, 111 Wash.2d 159 (1988)
- Building permits only authorize specific construction; they don't guarantee
  compliance with all codes
- But: This case was about *negligent permit issuance*, not nonconforming structures

#### City of Mercer Island v. Steinmann, 9 Wn. App. 479 (1973)
- Municipalities cannot be estopped from enforcing zoning by prior inaction or
  permit issuance
- Laches generally does not apply against government zoning enforcement

#### Rhod-A-Zalea v. Snohomish County, 136 Wn.2d 1 (1998)
- Nonconforming uses can lose status if expanded without permits
- A nonconforming use doesn't exempt from later-enacted building regulations

#### Total Outdoor Corp. v. City of Seattle, 187 Wn. App. 337 (2015)
- Referenced re: loss of nonconforming status if structure has been modified

## WHERE TO FIND PRE-1972 EVIDENCE

### 1. King County Assessor's Real Property Record Cards (1937-1972) [HIGHEST PRIORITY]

**What:** Cumulative history of every parcel in King County, including legal
descriptions, building use, construction details, and **at least one photograph**
per improved property.

**Why critical:** These cards cover EXACTLY the period needed (through 1972) and
include photos. If the dock existed during this period, it may be visible in the
property photograph or described in the building details.

**How to access:**
- **Online (digitized):** https://digitalarchives.wa.gov/Collections/TitleInfo/854
  - Search by parcel number: enter `220740-0080` (with hyphen between 6th and 7th
    digits, so try `220740-0080`)
  - Search by address: enter house number `9010` and street name `41st` (do NOT
    include direction or suffix)
- **In person:** Puget Sound Regional Archives, Bellevue, WA
  - Phone: (425) 564-3940
  - Email: psbrancharchives@sos.wa.gov

### 2. Historic Aerials - NETRonline [HIGH PRIORITY]

**What:** Interactive viewer with aerial imagery going back to the 1930s-1960s
for the Seattle metro area.

**How to access:**
- Go to https://www.historicaerials.com/viewer
- Enter address: 9010 NE 41st St, Yarrow Point, WA 98004
- Check timeline for available years (likely 1936, 1940, 1950, 1957, 1964, 1968,
  1970, 1974, etc.)
- Look for the dock/boat ramp structure along the waterfront
- **Take screenshots with timestamps** if dock is visible in pre-1972 imagery

### 3. USGS EarthExplorer [HIGH PRIORITY]

**What:** Free high-resolution aerial photography from 1937-2014, available for
download.

**How to access:**
1. Go to https://earthexplorer.usgs.gov/
2. Create free account (required for downloads)
3. Enter coordinates: ~47.6456 N, 122.2187 W (Yarrow Point area)
4. Set date range: 1960-1972 (or earlier)
5. Under Data Sets, expand "Aerial Imagery" > check "Aerial Photo Single Frames"
6. Download available imagery (free at medium resolution, $30/frame for high-res
   on-demand scans)

### 4. King County 1936 Aerial Photography [MODERATE PRIORITY]

**What:** Scanned 1936 aerial imagery for western King County, viewable online.

**How to access:**
- King County iMap: switch to "Aerial 1936" basemap and navigate to the property
- ArcGIS MapServer: https://gismaps.kingcounty.gov/arcgis/rest/services/BaseMaps/KingCo_Aerial_1936/MapServer
- ArcGIS Online: https://www.arcgis.com/home/item.html?id=eab8826a830644d48856d8debb5ff5ef

**Note:** 1936 is very early. The dock may not have existed yet, but if it did,
this would be extremely strong evidence.

### 5. King County Archives - Department of Public Works Photos (1968-1997) [HIGH PRIORITY]

**What:** Aerial photographs of parks and adjacent neighborhoods, mostly circa
1969-1975. Taken by staff photographer Bob Brittain.

**How to access:**
- Contact King County Archives
- Also: King County Public Works aerial photographs (Series 426) are indexed by
  street - check the index for NE 41st St

### 6. UW Libraries Aerial Photo Finder [MODERATE PRIORITY]

**What:** Map Collection has aerial photos for selected areas in WA, with emphasis
on Puget Sound region, from 1944 to present.

**How to access:**
- Use the finder tool: https://wagda.lib.washington.edu/Aerials/AerialsViewer.html
- Search for Yarrow Point area
- May need to visit UW Libraries Map Collection in person to view actual photos

### 7. Washington State Digital Archives - Yarrow Point Records [MODERATE PRIORITY]

**What:** Yarrow Point Town Council Ordinances (1959-2009) and Minutes (1959-2009).
These could contain records of dock permits, shoreline discussions, or building
activity on the property pre-1972.

**How to access:**

- Minutes: https://digitalarchives.wa.gov/Collections/TitleInfo/1534

**How to access:**

**How to access:**
