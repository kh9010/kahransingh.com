## 1. To touch, in a way

- **Date / location / time:** Mon Nov 24, 2025 — New York City — 01:21
- **Suggested slug:** `to-touch-in-a-way.html`
- **Suggested themes:** love, identity

```
To touch
in a way
gives remembrance
I don't know
if I can take

Sometimes
```

*Editorial note:* A tiny, bruised two-stanza lyric in the register of `to-know-me` and `delicate-pieces-before-the-end`. Clear fit — it reads as a whole, even at six lines.

## 2. Hey you there, hey you here

- **Date / location / time:** Wed Mar 26, 2025 — New York City — 06:09
- **Suggested slug:** `hey-you-there-hey-you-here.html`
- **Suggested themes:** love, identity

```
hey you there
hey you here
we got stuck together somehow
your shoulder got caught in my shirt
your breath got caught in my air
*beat*
is that mine or is that yours
*beat*
```

*Editorial note:* Intimate morning-after blur. Kin to `everytime-you-wink-at-me` and `falling-in-love`. The `*beat*` stage directions are unusual for the site — worth a read to decide whether to keep them, convert to white space, or drop.

## 3. Teental

- **Date / location / time:** Sat Dec 6, 2025 — Nelemanglam — 16:06
- **Suggested slug:** `teental.html`
- **Suggested themes:** identity, place, wonder

```
teental
ta comes the first step, to greet sum and kiss it goodbye, assure it the cycle begins again
te comes the second step, likely alone in the sound scape, a perfect (quarter/half/eight/third/what math can you not do) away from the beat to come
te right foot again, are we floating now or chasing the beat
te left and the cycle is half done, this time we will be strong

who am I a dancer who can't keep attention for 4 beats, where are we going to find heritage in a mélange of cultures, what search for heritage is this but someone to choose you to love you to tell you you are loved loved loved

ah left again, we've entered the back half, and if it was chogun we dance here has come the beat again, to ground us and remind us our math need be right every time
te right foot have you spoken as loud? Or 6th beat have you fallen soft, leaving the memory of the 4th and 3rd to leave a mark
te left again and we close in on home, sum, likely a foot away, unless this tihai has left us twisted and turned, math askew.
ta have we come home to you, sum? or is this a fake, a feint to remind the listener, watcher, life is a game, and nothing is what it seems.
```

*Editorial note:* A walk through the 16-beat Indian classical cycle that opens into a heritage-and-belonging question at the middle stanza. Nothing on the site currently does rhythm-as-form like this; kin to `kashmir` and `anuraag-oh-anuraag` in its India-register. Slightly longer and more technical than most site poems — consider whether glossing (sum, tihai, chogun) is needed.

a conversation among birds

## 5. Hey you tree there

- **Date / location / time:** Sat Dec 6, 2025 — Nelemanglam — 16:20
- **Suggested slug:** `hey-you-tree-there.html`
- **Suggested themes:** nature, wonder, place

```
Hey you tree there it's nice to see you today I didn't know you knew so much about height and heights -- I knew you knew about growth but it was only when I started to talk to this one part of you it told me about how we are always thinking about heights.
And how most of you doesn't have to worry about heights -- most of you gets to just grow and do its thing, be growing and be taller. Be trying to be ever taller. Responsible for the height, but not for worrying about it just growth growth growth.
Because of the other trees, you see, they have this way of coming in and like how -- blocking the light, eating the soil, you can't even find or make a grape of light, a square come to be a circle because 3d always renders weird in 2d, you think we meant to have our rings be this way? We were trying to just grow grow grow, and that made it much harder to worry about how we would render when you cut 2d segments out of us -- we never dreamed we would be required to be snapshots in half, mandolin'd into the thinnest slices possible sometimes, because those are the things that show them they know how to really do it, how to win at life, show them they know how to cut, how to get themselves a tree.
Hey you tree I didn't know what those gashes those gouges in your trunk came from are they a hurt from when you are young or are they a hurt from maintaining the base for all of the height you need to achieve to have a spot of light in this world.
Hey you tree I thought that was a little bit of poop that was on your bark on your trunk on your arm but what poop could stain a bark or a trunk or an arm of a tree? Is it a fungus my tree friend is that what left the scars the gouges not ants not termites not woodpeckers searching for a mate but was it a fungus that organism so mysterious how could something so small hold so much mystery, I hear its a story the elephant is asking its friends after it saw a mouse, have you ever seen an elephant actually frightened of a mouse, don't say it too loud or they will hear and start hunting and cut down all of us trees oops I mean you trees the hills the grass the arms the trunks all to find the elephant hiding from those, you know it's true you there tree my friend, you.
```

*Editorial note:* Long, run-on, monologic prose poem — significantly longer than the site's typical lyrics. Thematically it belongs beside `an-ode-to-aspens`, `have-you-watched-creepers-creep`, and `i-watched-a-butterfly-today`, but the length and prose form would be an outlier in the existing poetry grid. Primary concern: scale. Possible remedy is to let it live as-is and be the site's first long prose poem, or to excerpt.
